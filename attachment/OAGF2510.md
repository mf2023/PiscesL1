## Part I – General Provisions & Scope

### Clause 1  Purpose & Legal Basis

This Framework provides a global baseline for the governance of AI systems, ensuring:

1.1 **Human-centricity**: AI systems shall serve human well-being and societal development.  
1.2 **Risk prevention**: Organizations shall implement forward-looking risk identification and control.  
1.3 **Innovation friendliness**: This Framework shall not impose technical choices that hinder legitimate innovation.  
1.4 **Interoperability**: Cross-border deployment shall incur minimal duplicated compliance costs.

Legal sources include, inter alia, cyber-security, data-protection, consumer-protection, IP, and content-regulation instruments worldwide.

### Clause 2  Scope & Technical Definitions

2.1 **AI System Types** (non-exhaustive, registrable in Annex F):  
a. *generative-model* – produces novel data instances (e.g., text, image, audio, video, code, 3D models, scientific structures);  
b. *multimodal-model* – processes ≥2 modalities jointly;  
c. *reasoning-model* – performs logical or mathematical inference;  
d. *agent-system* – operates autonomously in pursuit of goals;  
e. *embedding-model* – maps data to vector space;  
f. *world-model* – simulates environments or systems for prediction and planning;  
g. *neuromorphic-system* – employs architectures inspired by biological neural networks;  
h. *reserved-type* – for future registration per Clause 2.5.

2.2 **Application Domains**: content generation, conversational agents, code synthesis, data analytics, automated decision-making, creative tooling, and any emerging domain registered under Annex H.

2.3 **Geographic Scope**: any jurisdiction; cross-border deployments shall comply with mapped rules per Annex G.

2.4 **Extensible Architecture**: This framework provides structured extensibility mechanisms for new technology types, jurisdictional requirements, industry applications, and risk red-lines through registration tables defined in normative annexes. Any extension shall:
a) Be proposed and community-reviewed through the `OAGF-RFC-NNNN` process;  
b) Be registered in the corresponding annex table with a unique identifier;  
c) Not modify or compromise the stability and compatibility of core clauses.

2.5 **Future Registration**: New AI system types not covered in Clause 2.1 shall be registered through the process defined in Clause 2.4 and Annex F.

### Clause 3  Fundamental Principles

3.1 **Human-centricity**: Organizations shall retain human final authority over decisions that significantly affect natural persons.

3.2 **Transparency**: Operators shall disclose, in plain language, algorithmic purpose, functionality, and material risks (see Annex D-3 template).

3.3 **Fairness**: Systems shall not discriminate on grounds of race, gender, age, religion, nationality, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

3.4 **Privacy-by-Design**: Default settings shall ensure the highest level of data protection; data minimisation shall be mandatory.

3.5 **Safety & Controllability**: Organisations shall implement lifecycle controls ensuring predictability, interruptibility, and correctability.

3.6 **Accountability**: A traceable responsibility chain shall be documented and made available to regulators within 72 h upon request.

### Clause 4  Risk-Based Management System

4.1 **Prohibited AI Practices** – techniques that contravene fundamental rights shall not be placed on the market (see https://dunimd.org/oagf/redline/1.0.json).  
4.2 **High-Risk Systems** – systems listed in Annex A Table A.1 shall undergo conformity assessment prior to deployment.  
4.3 **Limited-Risk Systems** – shall meet transparency obligations per Clause 17.  
4.4 **Minimal-Risk Systems** – shall follow the baseline checklist in Annex B.  
4.5 **Dynamic Risk Updates** – organisations shall re-evaluate risk class within 30 days of any material change in purpose, scale, or environment.

## Part II: Technical Compliance Requirements


### Clause 5  Data Governance & Privacy

5.1 **Lawful Basis**: Data shall be processed only under a documented lawful basis per Annex G-2.  
5.2 **Data Minimisation**: Controllers shall collect the minimum data adequate, relevant, and limited to the stated purpose.  
5.3 **Cross-Border Transfers**: Transfers shall comply with the adequacy list or approved transfer mechanism in Annex G-3.  
5.4 **Data-Subject Rights**: Systems shall provide automated means to access, rectify, erase, and export personal data within 30 days of request.  
5.5 **Security Safeguards**: Controllers shall implement the security baselines listed in Annex C and notify supervisory authorities of breaches within 72 h.

### Clause 6  Model Training Process Governance

6.1 **Training Data Compliance Management**
   a) **Data Source Verification**: Establish complete training data source traceability, including data collection methods, authorization status, copyright status, and quality assessment reports  
   b) **Personal Data Protection**: When training data contains personal information, implement anonymization or pseudonymization measures, ensure compliance with data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)  
   c) **Data Quality Control**: Establish data quality assessment standards, including data completeness, accuracy, timeliness, and fairness indicators  
   d) **Bias Detection and Mitigation**: Conduct bias analysis before model training, identify potential discriminatory factors, and take data balancing measures  
   e) **Training Data Audit**: Conduct quarterly audits of training data, retain audit records for at least 3 years  

6.2 **Model Alignment and Safety Management**
   a) **Value Alignment**: Ensure model output aligns with human values and ethical standards, establish value alignment evaluation metrics  
   b) **Safety Assessment**: Conduct comprehensive safety assessments before model release, including but not limited to harmful content generation, privacy leakage, and misuse risks  
   c) **Capability Threshold Management**: Trigger enhanced security measures and registration based on a multi-factor profile (e.g., training compute, parameter count, external tool execution, autonomous action scope, networking), with thresholds defined in Annex A-2  
   d) **Continuous Learning Management**: When models adopt continuous learning mechanisms, ensure the safety and compliance of new training data, prevent model performance degradation  

6.3 **Training Process Transparency Requirements**
   a) **Algorithm Transparency**: Disclose core algorithm principles, model architecture, and key technical parameters  
   b) **Training Process Documentation**: Record the complete training process, including hyperparameter settings, training duration, resource consumption, etc.  
   c) **Performance Evaluation Report**: Provide detailed model performance evaluation reports, including accuracy, recall, fairness indicators, etc.  
   d) **Third-Party Verification**: High-risk models should undergo algorithm filing and third-party security assessment  

6.4 **Generative Model Special Requirements**
   a) **Content Generation Control**: Establish content generation filtering mechanisms to prevent the generation of illegal, harmful, or discriminatory content  
   b) **Copyright Compliance**: Ensure training data and generated content do not infringe on third-party copyrights  
   c) **Watermark Technology**: Apply appropriate watermark technology to AI-generated content for traceability and identification  
   d) **User Usage Monitoring**: Monitor user usage of generative models, identify and prevent misuse  

6.5 **Deployment Validation Requirements**
   Prior to model release, the following validation work shall be completed:
   a) **Performance Benchmark Testing**: Pass performance benchmarks listed in Annex A-2  
   b) **Adversarial Robustness Testing**: Pass adversarial robustness tests per Annex C-3  
   c) **Safety Filter Integration**: Integrate safety filters per Clause 17 requirements  
   d) **Compliance Review**: Pass compliance reviews for data usage, algorithm design, and security measures  

6.6 **Post-Release Monitoring and Maintenance**
   a) **Output Quality Monitoring**: Continuously monitor model output quality, identify and handle quality drift issues  
   b) **Safety Incident Response**: Upon discovering safety incidents, initiate Clause 20 incident response mechanism within 24 hours  
   c) **Model Update Management**: When model updates or iterations are needed, ensure the safety and compliance of the update process  
   d) **User Feedback Handling**: Establish user feedback collection and processing mechanisms, continuously optimize model performance

### Clause 7  Inference Service Runtime Governance

7.1 **Real-time Decision Management**
   a) **Decision Audit Trail**: Record complete inference decision process, including input data, model parameters, decision logic, and output results  
   b) **Decision Latency Control**: Ensure inference service response time meets business requirements, set timeout mechanisms  
   c) **Decision Accuracy Monitoring**: Real-time monitoring of model inference accuracy, trigger alerts when accuracy drops below threshold  
   d) **Decision Rollback Mechanism**: Establish decision rollback mechanisms for critical decisions, allowing timely error correction  

7.2 **Service Stability and Reliability**
   a) **Service Availability Guarantee**: Ensure inference service availability is not less than 99.9%, establish fault tolerance and backup mechanisms  
   b) **Load Balancing Management**: Implement reasonable load balancing strategies to prevent service overload  
   c) **Fault Detection and Recovery**: Establish automated fault detection and recovery mechanisms to minimize service interruption time  
   d) **Performance Monitoring**: Comprehensive monitoring of service performance metrics, including response time, throughput, error rate, etc.  

7.3 **Dynamic Risk Control**
   a) **Real-time Risk Assessment**: Conduct risk assessment of each inference request, dynamically adjust security policies based on risk level  
   b) **Anomaly Detection**: Utilize anomaly detection algorithms to identify abnormal inference requests and outputs  
   c) **Dynamic Permission Management**: Dynamically adjust user permissions based on usage behavior and risk assessment results  
   d) **Emergency Response Mechanism**: Establish emergency response procedures for major security incidents, ensuring rapid incident handling  

7.4 **Reasoning & Multimodal Models Special Requirements**
   a) **Explainability**: Operators shall log key inference steps and provide human-readable explanations on request (template: Annex D-2)  
   b) **Consistency Check**: Systems shall implement automated checks for logical contradictions; detected errors shall be resolved before downstream use  
   c) **Attack Resistance**: Reasoning pipelines shall be validated against prompt-injection and adversarial-logic attacks per Annex C-4  
   d) **Cross-modal Consistency**: For multimodal models, ensure consistency and coordination between different modal inputs and outputs  

7.5 **Resource Management and Optimization**
   a) **Computing Resource Allocation**: Reasonably allocate computing resources to ensure efficient operation of inference services  
   b) **Cost Control**: Optimize resource utilization, control inference service costs  
   c) **Energy Efficiency Management**: Adopt energy-saving technologies to reduce energy consumption of inference services  
   d) **Resource Scaling**: Automatically adjust resource allocation based on load changes to achieve elastic scaling  

7.6 **API Security and Access Control**
   a) **Identity Authentication**: Implement strict identity authentication mechanisms to ensure only authorized users can access inference services  
   b) **Access Authorization**: Establish fine-grained access control policies based on the principle of least privilege  
   c) **API Rate Limiting**: Set reasonable API call frequency limits to prevent abuse  
   d) **Communication Encryption**: Encrypt all API communications using secure encryption protocols

### Clause 8  AI-Agent Systems

8.1 **Authorisation Matrix**: Agents shall operate only within pre-authorised action spaces; overrides shall require multi-factor approval.  
8.2 **Goal Alignment**: Organisations shall verify alignment between agent objectives and human intent, with drift detection every 30 days.  
8.3 **Multi-Agent Security**: Inter-agent messages shall be authenticated and rate-limited; malicious behaviour shall trigger Clause 20 incident-response.

## Part III: Data Governance & Privacy Protection


### Clause 9  Algorithmic Transparency & Explainability

9.1 **Disclosure Package**: Operators shall publish a machine-readable fact-sheet (schema: https://oagf.org/schema/2025/core.json) covering purpose, architecture, training-data summary, and known limitations.  
9.2 **User-Facing Explanation**: Systems shall provide concise explanations of individual decisions within 5 s of request. For complex decisions, up to 30 s is permissible; pre-generated or cached explanations may be used with queue logging.  
9.3 **Independent Audit**: High-risk systems shall undergo external conformity assessment every 12 months; audit summaries shall be made public.

### Clause 10  Personal Information Processing

10.1 **Lawful Bases**: Processing shall rely on one of the six lawful bases listed in Annex G-2; legitimate-interest assessments shall be documented.  
10.2 **Data-Subject Rights**: Controllers shall provide an API endpoint enabling users to access, rectify, erase, and port personal data within 30 days.  
10.3 **Cross-Border Transfers**: Transfers shall be subject to approved transfer-impact assessments and recorded in the register per Clause 5.3.

### Clause 11  Content Moderation & Filtering

11.1 **Prohibited Content Set**: Filters shall block content defined in Annex X-1 with graded thresholds per risk level and application context, as specified in Annex A-2; performance shall be validated under documented protocols.  
11.2 **Child Safety**: Systems likely to be accessed by minors shall implement age-verification and parental-control interfaces per Annex X-2.  
11.3 **User Reporting**: Operators shall maintain a 24 h reporting channel; confirmed violations shall be removed or restricted within 12 h.

### Clause 12  AI-Generated Content Labelling

12.1 **Synthetic Media**: AI-generated or manipulated image, audio, or video content shall carry machine-readable provenance metadata per Annex E-1; verification shall follow Annex E-3.  
12.2 **Text Disclosure**: Chat or text outputs shall include a conspicuous notice when the content is AI-generated (template: Annex E-2).  
12.3 **Cryptographic Signature**: High-impact synthetic media shall be signed with an authorised certificate listed in Annex E-3.

## Part IV: Algorithm Governance & Transparency

### Clause 13  Record-Keeping & Impact Assessments

13.1 **Risk Register**: Operators shall maintain a live risk register covering Clause 4 classifications, mitigations, and residual risk (template: Annex A-3).  
13.2 **Data-Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)**: High-risk systems shall complete a DPIA before deployment and review it annually; the DPIA shall be made available to the competent authority on request.  
13.3 **Fundamental-Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA)**: Systems with potential impact on fundamental rights shall undergo a FRIA per Annex A-4; summary findings shall be published.

### Clause 14  Bias Detection & Mitigation

14.1 **Test Datasets**: Organisations shall curate test sets that are representative across gender, race, age, and other protected attributes; dataset cards shall be published per https://oagf.org/test/bias-2025.csv.  
14.2 **Metrics & Thresholds**: Bias metrics (e.g., equal-opportunity difference) shall not exceed the thresholds set in Annex A-5; violations shall trigger re-training or decommissioning.  
14.3 **Corrective Actions**: Detected bias shall be mitigated by data augmentation, algorithmic debiasing, or post-processing; effectiveness shall be re-validated before re-deployment.

### Clause 15  Explainability Requirements

15.1 **Global Explanation**: Operators shall publish a model card (Annex D-5) describing design choices, data sources, and ethical considerations.  
15.2 **Local Explanation**: For high-risk decisions, systems shall produce counterfactual or feature-attribution explanations within 5 s. For complex decisions, up to 30 s is permissible; pre-generated or cached explanations may be used with queue logging.  
15.3 **Explanation Accuracy**: Explanations shall be validated against ground-truth decision logic; < 5 % divergence is permissible.

### Clause 16  Human Oversight

16.1 **Design-in Oversight**: High-risk systems shall embed human-review checkpoints that cannot be bypassed by default.  
16.2 **Override Authority**: Human operators shall have authority to stop, override, or reverse any system decision within a 5-minute SLA.  
16.3 **Competency & Training**: Organisations shall ensure oversight staff are competent and re-certified annually; training records shall be retained for 3 years.

## Part V: Content Governance & Labeling Obligations

### Clause 17  Security & Resilience

17.1 **Adversarial Testing**: Operators shall subject high-risk systems to red-team exercises at least annually; findings shall be remediated within 30 days.  
17.2 **Fail-Safe Defaults**: Systems shall degrade gracefully under fault conditions; critical functions shall remain available or shut down safely.  
17.3 **Supply-Chain Security**: Third-party components shall be sourced only from vendors that meet the baseline controls in Annex C-5; SBOMs shall be maintained.

### Clause 18  Incident Response & Business Continuity

18.1 **Incident Classification**: Events shall be classified within 1 h using the severity matrix in Annex B-1.  
18.2 **Notification**: Serious incidents shall be reported to the competent authority and affected users within 72 h.  
18.3 **Recovery**: Operators shall maintain and test a business-continuity plan that restores critical services within RTO defined in Annex B-2.

### Clause 19  IP & Copyright

19.1 **Training Clearance**: Organisations shall maintain licences or valid exceptions for all copyrighted works used in training; evidence shall be stored for 3 years.  
19.2 **Output Screening**: Systems shall implement pre-publication screening to detect and suppress substantially similar reproductions of protected works (threshold: Annex A-6).  
19.3 **Redress Mechanism**: Operators shall provide a 24 h notice-and-takedown procedure; confirmed violations shall be resolved within 7 days.

### Clause 20  Disinformation & Authenticity

20.1 **Provenance Metadata**: Operators shall attach cryptographic provenance metadata to any synthetic media intended for public dissemination (format: Annex E-1). Verification and trust anchors shall follow Annex E-3, including certificate revocation handling.  
20.2 **Source Transparency**: When AI is used to edit or summarise third-party content, the original source URL and modification log shall be displayed.  
20.3 **Rapid Correction**: Upon verification of disinformation, operators shall propagate corrections with equal algorithmic weight within 2 h.

## Part VI: Liability & Remedies
Applicability: These provisions apply when adopted by a jurisdiction or under the OAGF Conformant certification framework; otherwise they serve as a non-binding industry baseline.

### Clause 21  User Rights

21.1 **Right to Information**: Controllers shall provide a concise, 250-word description of AI logic, data used, and potential impacts at the point of first interaction.  
21.2 **Right to Opt-Out**: Users shall be able to withdraw consent or object to processing without detriment; a human alternative shall be available for high-risk decisions.  
21.3 **Right to Complain**: Operators shall provide a single digital entry point for complaints; substantive responses shall be issued within 30 days.

### Clause 22  Liability Allocation

22.1 **Strict Liability for Prohibited Practices**: Any operator that places a prohibited AI practice (Clause 4.1) on the market shall be strictly liable for all resulting damages.  
22.2 **Fault-Based Liability**: For other breaches, liability shall depend on proof of failure to meet the obligations of this Guide; contributory negligence of the claimant may reduce damages.  
22.3 **Insurance & Guarantees**: Operators of high-risk systems shall maintain financial-security instruments (minimum cover: Annex A-7).

### Clause 23  Redress & Remedies

23.1 **Compensation**: Any natural or legal person who has suffered harm due to a breach of this Guide shall be entitled to full and effective compensation.  
23.2 **Injunctions**: Courts may order cessation or remediation of non-compliant practices; interim relief shall be available where irreparable harm is likely.  
23.3 **Collective Actions**: Representative bodies may bring collective actions for widespread harm; damages may be aggregated and distributed pro-rata.

### Clause 24  Final Provisions

24.1 **Confidentiality**: Trade secrets and personal data contained in compliance documentation shall be handled per Annex G-4.  
24.2 **Review Cycle**: This Guide shall be reviewed at least every 24 months by the Maintenance Body; proposed amendments shall be published for public comment for 90 days.  
24.3 **Transitional Measures**: Existing systems shall achieve conformity within 18 months of publication; deviations shall be documented in a transition plan approved by the competent authority.  
24.4 **Extension Registry**: 本规范通过 Annex F / G / H / X 为新技术、新法域、新行业、新红线预留注册入口；任何扩展 shall not 修改 Core 条款编号，仅通过 OAGF-RFC-NNNN 流程登记。

## ANNEX C: BEST PRACTICE GUIDELINES

### Technical Architecture Best Practices
1. **Modular Design**: Adopt modular architecture for easy compliance function integration and updates
   - Implementation points: Decompose AI system into independent functional modules (data preprocessing, model training, inference service, user interface, etc.), each module having clear input/output interfaces and responsibility boundaries
   - Technical standards: Follow ISO/IEC 25010 software quality standards, ensure loose coupling and high cohesion between modules
   - Documentation requirements: Maintain detailed technical documentation for each module, including function description, interface specifications, dependency relationships, etc.

2. **Built-in Explainability**: Consider explainability requirements during system design phase
   - Technical implementation: Integrate explainability algorithms such as LIME, SHAP, ensure model decision-making process is traceable
   - Architecture requirements: Establish explanation service layer, independent of core inference engine, support multiple explanation methods
   - Performance indicators: Explanation response time ≤5 seconds, explanation accuracy ≥95%, user understanding score ≥4.0/5.0

3. **Privacy Protection by Default**: Enable highest level of privacy protection measures by default
   - Technical measures: Implement differential privacy (ε≤1.0), federated learning, homomorphic encryption and other privacy protection technologies
   - Data management: Establish data classification system, sensitive data encrypted with AES-256 storage
   - Access control: Implement role-based access control (RBAC), support principle of least privilege

4. **Security Mechanism Redundancy**: Establish multi-level security protection mechanisms
   - Network security: Deploy WAF, DDoS protection, intrusion detection systems (IDS)
   - Application security: Implement OWASP Top 10 protection measures, conduct regular vulnerability scans
   - Data security: Establish data backup and recovery mechanism, RPO≤15 minutes, RTO≤2 hours

5. **Complete Audit Logs**: Record all critical operations and decision-making processes
   - Log standards: Follow RFC 5424 system log protocol, ensure log integrity and tamper-proof
   - Record content: Include user identity, operation time, operation type, input data, output results, exception information
   - Storage requirements: Log retention ≥3 years, support fast retrieval and audit analysis, query response time ≤30 seconds

### Operational Management Best Practices
1. **Cross-department Collaboration**: Establish collaboration mechanisms between legal, technical, and business departments
   - Organizational structure: Establish AI Governance Committee, chaired by C-level executives, including legal, technical, and business representatives
   - Meeting mechanisms: Hold monthly compliance meetings, quarterly risk assessment meetings, annual compliance audits
   - Decision-making process: Establish three-level decision-making mechanism (daily compliance, major risks, crisis response), clarify decision-making authority and time limits

2. **External Expert Consultation**: Regularly consult legal and technical expert opinions
   - Expert network: Establish external expert pool, including legal, ethical, technical, and industry experts ≥20 people
   - Consultation frequency: Legal experts monthly consultation, technical experts quarterly review, ethical experts semi-annual assessment
   - Consultation records: Establish expert consultation files, including consultation questions, expert suggestions, adoption status, implementation effects

3. **User Education**: Provide users with compliance usage guidance and training
   - Training content: AI system function introduction, compliance usage guidelines, risk warnings, rights protection
   - Training forms: Online tutorials, video demonstrations, interactive guides, on-site training
   - Effect evaluation: User training completion rate ≥90%, satisfaction score ≥4.0/5.0, compliance violation rate reduction ≥50%

4. **Supplier Management**: Conduct compliance assessment of suppliers and partners
   - Assessment standards: Establish supplier compliance assessment system, including technical capabilities, compliance level, risk control and other dimensions
   - Access mechanism: Implement supplier access review, require compliance certificates, security certifications, insurance guarantees
   - Continuous monitoring: Conduct quarterly supplier compliance reviews, establish blacklist mechanism, prohibit cooperation with violating suppliers for 3 years

5. **Continuous Monitoring**: Establish 7×24-hour system monitoring mechanism
   - Monitoring scope: System performance, security incidents, compliance violations, user behavior, abnormal situations
   - Alert mechanism: Establish multi-level alert system, respond to critical events within 5 minutes, escalate major events within 15 minutes
   - Monitoring data: Establish monitoring data analysis platform, support real-time analysis and historical trend analysis

### Risk Control Best Practices
1. **Forward-looking Assessment**: Conduct sufficient compliance assessment before new technology application
   - Assessment timing: Start at requirement analysis phase, complete at design review phase, verify before launch
   - Assessment content: Technical risks, legal risks, ethical risks, social risks, business risks
   - Assessment methods: Use combination of qualitative and quantitative methods, establish risk assessment matrix and impact analysis model

2. **Scenario-based Analysis**: Develop specialized compliance solutions for different application scenarios
   - Scenario classification: Classify by industry (finance, healthcare, education, transportation, etc.) and application type (recommendation, decision-making, generation, etc.)
   - Risk characteristics: Identify special risk points for each scenario, such as fairness requirements for financial scenarios, safety requirements for medical scenarios
   - Control measures: Develop targeted risk control measures, such as algorithm fairness testing for financial scenarios, safety verification for medical scenarios

3. **Stress Testing**: Regularly conduct compliance stress testing under extreme conditions
   - Testing frequency: Conduct regular stress testing quarterly, extreme stress testing annually
   - Testing scenarios: Including high concurrency, data anomalies, network attacks, system failures, compliance violations and other extreme situations
   - Testing indicators: System availability ≥99.9%, data integrity 100%, compliance violation detection rate ≥95%

4. **Emergency Drills**: Regularly conduct security incident emergency drills
   - Drill types: Data breach drills, system attack drills, compliance violation drills, service interruption drills
   - Drill frequency: Conduct tabletop exercises monthly, practical drills quarterly, comprehensive drills annually
   - Drill evaluation: Establish drill evaluation system, including response time, handling effectiveness, collaboration efficiency, improvement suggestions

5. **Experience Summary**: Establish compliance experience summary and sharing mechanism
   - Knowledge base: Establish compliance knowledge base, including regulatory interpretation, case analysis, best practices, lessons learned
   - Sharing mechanism: Regularly hold compliance sharing sessions, encourage employees to share compliance experiences and innovative practices
